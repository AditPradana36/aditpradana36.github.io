On-going Projects

Spatial Modeling of Visual Element Proportion Diversity in Urban Spaces Based on Street-view and Spatial Indicators

This project examines the spatial distribution and diversity of urban visual elements using street-view imagery and spatial indicators. Combining computer vision, GIS analysis, and urban design, it quantifies greenness, openness, and enclosure to model spatial patterns and inform data-driven, climate-resilient urban planning.

Cooling Cities, Warming Communities: Quantifying Climate Resilience in Urban Parks Through Anthropomorphic Mapping and Place Attachment

Investigating climate resilience in urban green spaces through the integration of anthropomorphic mapping and place attachment analysis. The project aims to bridge emotional geographies and spatial planning to support adaptive urban design and urban landscape.

Previous Projects

Level of Protection of Fire-Prone Areas in East Jakarta City

Evaluated the spatial vulnerability of fire-prone areas in East Jakarta and assessed the adequacy of existing protection infrastructure. Using GIS and spatial-statistical methods, the study identified high-risk zones and provided data-driven recommendations to improve urban fire resilience. The findings were published in a peer-reviewed research article.
