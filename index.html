About Me

I am a Research Assistant at the Smart City Universitas Indonesia. With a strong foundation in Geography and expertise in Geospatial Analytics, I focus on bridging spatial data science, urban design, and planning to support sustainable urban development. My work emphasizes translating data-driven insights into practical strategies for resilient and equitable cities.

I earned both my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Geography from Universitas Indonesia. I aim to understand how people perceive and engage with space to inform inclusive, accessible, and adaptive planning processes rooted in spatial intelligence.

Research Interests

My research interest lies in understanding how human perspective and visual experience are embedded within urban spatial structures. I am particularly intrigued by how spatial configurations, as seen through the human eye, shape behavior, perception, and inclusivity in cities.

  • Urban Spatial Approach: I apply spatial thinking and geographic analysis to explore the morphology, structure, and transformation of urban areas. This approach enables the examination of urban inequality, spatial patterns, and policy implications through geostatistical modeling and spatial simulation. By utilizing neighborhood-scale diagnostics up to metropolitan-level assessments, I focus on integrating spatial knowledge into urban planning and sustainable development practices.
  • Street-View Imagery-Based Analytics: My research utilizes street-level imagery, semantic segmentation, and machine learning to extract and analyze visual components of urban environments. I focus on modeling visual experience elements such as greenness, openness, and enclosure from the human-eye perspective. These analytics aim to bridge perception-based urban theory with scalable spatial data to support inclusive, pedestrian-friendly, and equitable public space planning.
  • GIS and Remote Sensing as Fundamental Tools: Geographic Information Systems and Remote Sensing serve as the foundational technologies in my research. I use remote sensing for land classification, urban monitoring, and environmental assessments, while GIS integrates multi-layered spatial data for modeling, analysis, and visualization. This combination supports robust urban diagnostics, spatial accessibility studies, and evidence-based planning and design.
